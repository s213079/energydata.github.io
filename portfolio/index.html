The Paris Agreement is a global climate change agreement which was established with the long-term goal of limiting the increase of global warming to below a threshold of 2 degrees celsius. Such an agreement pushes countries to move toward rapid reductions in greenhouse gas emissions - with some leniency towards less developed countries

Here we hope to analyze the progress of each country in reducing greenhouse gas emissions and assess the current use of each energy type by country. It is also of interest to observe how impactful these reductions are to country GDP growth.

Using a combination of three data sets, the following visualizations documenting change in emission types, current consumption types, and impact on GDP are displayed for each country. The Paris Agreement was first signed in December 2015 and it has been in effect since October 2016 so the analysis will take place from 2015 - 2020.
